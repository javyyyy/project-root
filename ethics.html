Generative AI FAQ: The Ethical Implications!

In academic settings, such as where you have to write theses and research papers, it is important to know that AI is not objective when it comes to opinions, and has no concept of accountability. This means that whatever information the artificial intelligence acquires from the internet and language corpora means that the responsibility of using such information falls upon the person using the artificial intelligence. The artificial intelligence takes no responsibility for the usage of information, and is obligated to disclose itself being used when it comes to academic outputs such as theses.

Because the usage of AI doesn’t explicitly cite the authors of the works they reference from, it is important that the student using the artificial intelligence verifies the source and is able to cite it directly and properly. It is important to maintain transparency by being able to cite what the sources are and their relevance to the output when artificial intelligence takes from them.

Artificial intelligence is immensely prone to giving inaccurate and biased information. Thus, it falls upon the student using the artificial intelligence to make sure that all sources are accurate and provide factual data, and ensuring that information used is not invalid due to being outdated. To start on making sure that the information is valid, it is important to ensure that the information used has a direct author and is credible with qualifications, and making sure that the paper, article or site used has its information being published within the last 10 years. Anything that does not fall under these qualifications should not be used if the artificial intelligence just so happens to dig them up.

The information used by Generative AI also references the possible biases that may come with the source, thus this means that it falls upon the student’s responsibility to ensure that information is objective and devoid of any biases or discriminatory information. ​

  • Use AI as an assistant, not a substitute for critical thinking and original work
  • Familiarize yourself with institutional policies and cite AI tools consistently and transparently
  • Always review, revise, and personalize AI-generated content to fit your unique voice and context
  • Verify all AI outputs with legitimate sources before incorporation
  • Use plagiarism detection tools proactively and avoid deceptive practices to mask AI involvement
  • Educate yourself and others on AI’s capabilities, limitations, biases, and ethical challenges
