Generative AI FAQ: What There is to Know!

Generative AI is a type of artificial intelligence that can make original content such as videos, images, text and code when requested by prompts from the user.

Generative AI is able to produce content by ‚Äútraining‚Äù itself by directly referencing from the internet. Once the artificial intelligence is able to take those references, it ‚Äúfine tunes‚Äù itself by the model feeding itself the data for a specific type of task. Because the AI is moreso-suited for general types of tasks, it has to fine tune itself to specialize in the task the user is requesting for, hence the fine-tuning.

The most notable examples of generative AI are large language models (LLMS) like ChatGPT by OpenAI, Gemini by Google, Claude by Opus. These are all diffusion models that derive their data and training from language corpora (defined as a large collection of texts stored on the internet, primarily for archive uses).

Generative AI‚Äôs main strength is in its convenience. While certain tasks may require manpower such as writing, coding and illustrating, Generative AI is able to create these tasks through automation, reducing the time taken to complete tasks and making workflow efficient. While most of the time it may not be able to compare to the skill level and intricacy of human-made works, its strength lies in its speed and its ability to complete less difficult things and allowing humans to complete the more complex ones.

While generative AI can be very convenient, it does contain a few risks in certain aspects, particularly in terms of ethics and overreliance. Overreliance on generative AI can lead to a decrease in productivity, particularly when it comes to motivation. Additionally, it also raises the questions on morality and accountability when used in academic settings, such as theses and other types of academic papers. AI has already been used in such settings before, but there has not been any established rules yet, thus those who prompt the AI to create such generative content must take into account the use of AI means they are taking responsibility and ensuring that the content is objective and fit for different types of settings.
